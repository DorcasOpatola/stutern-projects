Egusi and Amala


Origin

Egusi (a Yoruba word) is the name for the protein-rich seeds of melon plants, which after being dried and ground is used as a major ingredient in West African cuisine. Major egusi-growing countries include Nigeria, Ghana, Burkina Faso, Togo, Cote d'Ivoire, Benin, Mali and Cameroon.
Amala is an indigenous Yoruba food, native to the Yoruba ethnic group of Southwestern Nigeria. It can be made of Yam flour, Cassava flour, or unripe Plantain flour.

Ingredients

Egusi Measurement
Ground Melon powder 100 g
Tomatoes 10 pieces
Bell peppers 6 pieces
Rodo 10 pieces
Palm oil 500 ml
Vegetables (Spinach, Bitter leaves or Pumkin leaves) As desired
Stock fish and Assortments
Chicken 1 kg
Grilled Catfish 500 g
Crayfish 30 g
Ponmo 500g
Snail As desired
Spices
Onions Two large pieces
Locust beans (Iru) As desired
Salt Add to taste
Amala Measurement
Yam flour 1 kg
Water 1 litre

Method of Preparation

Egusi: Add the tomatoes, rodo, and bell peppers into your blender. Add a fourth of the onions and blend. Set aside.

Cut up the chicken into sizes and put it into a pot. Add meat seasoning, some salt, and another fourth part of the onions. Bring it to boil. Add up the other stock fish and assortments. Let it simmer properly, then set aside.

Put the egusi into a bowl. Add a fourth of the onions. Mix it up. Add water in bits and mix to form a thick paste.

Into a clean dry pot, add the palm oil. Add in the last fourth of the diced onions. Pour in the blended pepper. Cover the pot and let it simmer till it starts to fry. Using a tablespoon, add in the egusi as little balls into the stew.

Stir frequently to prevent it from burning. Keep stirring till the egusi completely absorbs the stew (the contents of your pot should look like scrambled eggs).

Add the crayfish and iru and stir. Pour in the stock and assortments. Add salt to your taste. Cover it up and let it simmer.

Lastly, add in your vegetables as desired and let it simmer for a while. Your Egusi soup is ready.

Ensure your cooker is on low heat, egusi tends to burn easily when under high heat.

Amala: Pour in the water into a clean pot and let it boil. Add in your yam flourgently, sstir as you add till you get a consistent paste. Add in more hot water and cover it up to boil and cook properly. Stir to form consistent mix. Your Amala is ready to be served!
